Featured Research Project

🧪 Defending Your Dermis

A Comparative Study of Organic and Synthetic Sunscreens

Our project investigated the effectiveness of commercial and homemade sunscreens in protecting against UV radiation. This research was driven by the importance of skin health and the need to understand how different formulations provide protection from harmful UV rays.

🔬 Experimental Design

We carefully designed controlled experiments to ensure reliable and accurate results. Variables such as UV exposure time, sunscreen application thickness, and environmental conditions were meticulously controlled to isolate the effects of sunscreen type.

📊 Data Analysis

We collected UV protection ratings from multiple trials and analysed trends using statistical methods. Our data revealed insights into the efficacy of different sunscreen formulations across varying conditions.

🧠 Scientific Thinking

Throughout the project, we used evidence-based reasoning instead of assumptions. Our conclusions were grounded in empirical data, demonstrating rigorous scientific methodology and critical analysis.

Key Findings:

  • Commercial sunscreens showed consistent UV protection across multiple exposures
  • Homemade formulations provided variable protection levels
  • Application thickness significantly affects protective efficacy
  • Organic ingredients require different concentration levels than synthetic alternatives
